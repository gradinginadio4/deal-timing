Deal Timing in Equipment Sales

A Common Observation
Many equipment deals follow a natural rhythm: initial enthusiasm, technical evaluation, then a quieter period around months 4-6 when client-side priorities shift. This isn't failure—it's normal project dynamics.
Typical flow:
M1-2
M3
M4-6
M7+

Months 4-6 often see slower communication as clients navigate internal decisions

The question isn't whether deals slow down—most do. The question is how to maintain appropriate connection during these periods without pressure. Some companies have developed gentle, respectful approaches.

Thoughtful Follow-Up Approaches

Antwerp-based distributor
Instead of repeated "checking in" emails, they share relevant industry updates—regulatory changes, new efficiency standards—that genuinely help the client's planning. Positioned as advisor, not vendor.
Brussels-based integrator
Mapped typical client decision timelines and aligned touchpoints accordingly. Month 4: budget confirmation. Month 5: technical validation. Month 6: implementation planning. Each contact had specific purpose.
Ghent-based distributor
When deals stalled, they offered value-add conversations: "Even if timing has shifted, would a brief technical review be useful for your planning?" Many clients appreciated the no-pressure approach and re-engaged.
